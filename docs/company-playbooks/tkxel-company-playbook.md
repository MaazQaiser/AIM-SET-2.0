# Tkxel Company Playbook for DC Copilot

Source freshness: Official tkxel.com pages reviewed on June 7, 2026.

Audience: account executives, sales engineers, delivery leads, and AI copilots.

Purpose: give the copilot a grounded company-process layer it can use when users ask about Tkxel capabilities, engagement models, delivery approach, proposal flow, industries, AI governance, or common live-call questions.

Grounding rule: answer only from confirmed public source material or explicitly marked internal playbook material. Do not invent pricing, payment terms, legal commitments, SLA terms, discounting, or customer references.

## Executive Answer Card

Tkxel positioning: Tkxel is an AI-native strategic technology partner that modernizes systems, integrates AI, and builds custom software for measurable business impact.

Primary value themes:

- Modernize legacy systems into scalable, AI-ready foundations.
- Connect AI to real workflows and decision points, not just experiments.
- Build custom software, cloud, data, product, security, and operations capabilities.
- Use flexible engagement models that match scope clarity, control needs, budget structure, and scale.
- Reduce delivery ambiguity through discovery, project chartering, phased validation, and transparent governance.

Best short answer for prospects:

Tkxel helps companies move from idea to execution across AI, software engineering, cloud, data, cybersecurity, and business operations. The right engagement model depends on the client need: fixed-price for clear scope, dedicated teams for long-term control, offshore development centers for scalable capacity, and hybrid delivery when the client needs high-touch coordination plus offshore efficiency.

## Engagement Models

### Fixed-price or project-based

Use when:

- Scope is clear.
- Requirements are stable.
- Timeline and deliverables are predictable.
- The client needs cost certainty.
- The project is small to medium-sized or has tightly defined outcomes.

Talk track:

For a well-defined scope, fixed-price gives cost certainty and structured delivery. We agree specifications up front, then execute against the approved timeline, estimates, and deliverables.

### Dedicated team

Use when:

- The client needs ongoing product development.
- Priorities will evolve.
- The client wants direct oversight.
- Long-term continuity matters.
- They want specialists without recruitment or HR overhead.

Talk track:

A dedicated Tkxel team works as an extension of your internal team. You retain day-to-day priority control while getting scalable access to engineers, designers, architects, QA, and delivery expertise.

### Offshore development center / offshore development services

Use when:

- The client wants scalable capacity at lower overhead.
- They need specialized skills or faster turnaround.
- They want predictable, cost-efficient engineering support.
- They have enough internal ownership to coordinate with an extended team.

Talk track:

An offshore development center gives you scalable engineering capacity without expanding fixed internal overhead. The team can operate as an extension of your in-house group, improving delivery velocity and coverage across time zones.

### Hybrid delivery model

Use when:

- The client needs close collaboration or on-site/near-shore coordination.
- The client also wants offshore efficiency.
- Stakeholder alignment and cost efficiency both matter.
- Delivery requires ongoing support across time zones.

Talk track:

Hybrid delivery balances high-touch collaboration with offshore execution. It is useful when stakeholders need close alignment but also need scalable delivery capacity and optimized cost.

### Time and material

Use when:

- Scope is evolving.
- Requirements are not fully known.
- The client wants flexibility and active iteration.

Boundary:

The engagement page FAQ lists Time and Material as a common model, but the main public model descriptions emphasize fixed-price, dedicated team, offshore development, and hybrid. Treat Time and Material as available only if internal sales/commercial policy confirms it.

## Proposal and Onboarding Process

### Public proposal flow

When a prospect submits a request:

1. Tkxel reviews the request.
2. Tkxel keeps information private and can sign an NDA if needed.
3. Tkxel sends a proposal with timelines and estimates.

Recommended AE answer:

Once we understand your requirements, our experts review the scope, protect sensitive information under NDA if needed, and prepare a clear proposal with timelines and estimates. If the scope is still uncertain, we can start with discovery or feasibility to reduce risk before committing to a build plan.

### Post-contract onboarding flow

Once the contract is signed:

1. Contract confirmation and project research.
2. Project charter finalization and delivery team alignment.
3. Client kickoff and delivery transition.

The project charter captures client background, objectives, financial details, contractual terms, points of contact, scope, timelines, responsibilities, and operational context as a single source of truth.

Recommended AE answer:

After signature, we move into structured onboarding. We research the client context, finalize the project charter, align the delivery team on scope/timelines/responsibilities, and run a kickoff where teams agree communication plans, workflows, governance, and transition into delivery.

## Delivery Approach

Tkxel delivery principles:

- Partner with the client, not as a vendor.
- Ground decisions in client goals, constraints, operating environment, and measurable outcomes.
- Use transparent communication, realistic commitments, and shared accountability.
- Apply AI practically where it improves real workflows, decisions, automation, or product capability.
- Use pilots and MVPs to validate assumptions early and reduce delivery/investment risk.
- Define success by business impact, not activity alone.
- Keep progress, risks, and decisions visible to stakeholders.
- Manage change deliberately so delivery stays aligned as priorities evolve.

Delivery strengths:

- Experienced teams across engineering, design, architecture, QA, cloud, security, and AI.
- Complete project ownership from day one.
- Strategic development planning.
- Scope definition, cost evaluation, technical decision-making, and solution architecture guidance.

## AI-First Positioning

Tkxel's AI-first stance:

- AI should expand human capability.
- AI should be applied where it improves decision quality, speed, consistency, automation, product capability, or operational cost.
- AI should fit real workflows and decision points teams already use.
- AI should be governed from day one, with security, cost, and accountability considered early.

AI lifecycle support:

- AI strategy and direction.
- AI validation and early execution.
- AI-enabled operations.
- AI-powered products and platforms.

AI governance position:

- Maintain human oversight where AI informs decisions.
- Define data boundaries, usage policies, and review mechanisms.
- Align governance with standards including NIST AI Risk Management Framework, ISO 27001, and the EU AI Act.
- Support people transformation through role-based training, AI skill-gap assessment, workflow-level integration, and internal AI champions.

Recommended AE answer:

Our AI-first approach is practical. We do not add AI everywhere. We identify the workflows, decisions, and product capabilities where intelligence creates measurable value, then validate, govern, and scale the solution responsibly.

## Live-Call Talk Tracks

### If the client asks: "What are your standard engagement models?"

Answer:

We usually map the model to scope clarity and control needs:

- Fixed-price/project-based for clear scope and predictable timelines.
- Dedicated team when you need long-term specialists working as an extension of your team.
- Offshore development center when you need scalable capacity and lower overhead.
- Hybrid delivery when you need close collaboration plus offshore execution.

Then ask:

Which matters more for this initiative: cost certainty, speed to ramp, day-to-day control, or flexibility as scope evolves?

### If the client asks: "How do proposals work?"

Answer:

First, we review the requirement and clarify scope. If sensitive details are involved, we can work under NDA. Then we prepare a proposal with timelines and estimates. If there is ambiguity, we may recommend discovery or feasibility first so the proposal is realistic and lower risk.

Then ask:

Do you want the proposal optimized for a fixed scope, a pilot/MVP, or a longer-term team model?

### If the client asks: "What are your payment terms?"

Answer:

Payment and commercial terms are contract-specific and should be confirmed in the proposal, MSA, or SOW. What I can do now is help define the scope and engagement model so finance/legal can attach the right commercial structure.

Then ask:

Would it help if we separate the technical scope from the commercial assumptions in the next proposal draft?

### If the client asks: "Why partner instead of build in-house?"

Answer:

The strongest reasons are speed to validated delivery, access to specialized engineering/AI/cloud/security skills, reduced recruitment and HR overhead, flexible scaling, and structured governance across delivery. For evolving products, a dedicated or hybrid team can extend your internal team without forcing you to hire every capability permanently.

Then ask:

Which internal constraint is the biggest today: hiring speed, technical depth, delivery bandwidth, or governance risk?

### If the client asks: "Can you support AI safely?"

Answer:

Tkxel's AI-first approach emphasizes human oversight, data boundaries, usage policies, review mechanisms, and governance aligned with NIST AI RMF, ISO 27001, and the EU AI Act. We focus AI on real workflows and measurable outcomes, not experiments for their own sake.

Then ask:

Is your main AI concern value validation, data readiness, security, adoption, or governance?

### If the client asks: "Do you handle finance or back-office operations?"

Answer:

Tkxel has a business enablement layer that includes accounts and finance outsourcing, HR and recruitment process outsourcing, back-office process automation, and related operational support. For finance operations specifically, public service material covers bookkeeping, AP/AR, billing support, payroll processing, reporting, reconciliation, and cash-flow workflows.

Then ask:

Are you looking to outsource the finance operation itself, automate the workflow, or integrate finance data into reporting and analytics?
